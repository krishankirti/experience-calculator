Calories in Coffee with Milk: The Basics

The calorie count in coffee with milk depends on several factors, including:

  • Type of milk used (whole, skim, almond, soy, etc.)
  • Quantity of milk added
  • Additives like sugar or sweeteners

On average:

  • Black coffee: 2-5 calories per cup (240 ml)
  • Coffee with a splash of whole milk: Approximately 20-30 calories
  • Coffee with skim milk: Around 10-15 calories
  • Coffee with almond milk (unsweetened): About 5-10 calories
  • Coffee with soy milk (unsweetened): Roughly 15-20 calories

Detailed Breakdown: Milk Types and Their Impact

Whole Milk: A tablespoon adds around 9 calories to your coffee. Half a cup adds about 75 calories.

Skim Milk: A lower-calorie option with about 5 calories per tablespoon or 40 calories per half cup.

Plant-Based Milks:

  • Almond Milk (Unsweetened): 5-7 calories per tablespoon or 30 calories per half cup.
  • Soy Milk (Unsweetened): 10-15 calories per tablespoon or 60 calories per half cup.

How Does Sugar Impact Calories?

Adding sugar increases calories. One teaspoon of sugar adds 16 calories, which can quickly increase your coffee’s calorie count.

Health Benefits of Coffee with Milk

  • Rich in Calcium and Vitamin D: Milk provides essential nutrients for bone health.
  • Boosts Energy: The combination of caffeine and milk offers a balanced energy boost.
  • Enhanced Satiety: Milk in coffee can help curb hunger between meals.

Tips to Enjoy Low-Calorie Coffee with Milk

  • Measure your milk portions to avoid overpouring.
  • Opt for low-fat or plant-based options like skim milk or almond milk.
  • Avoid added sugars; use natural sweeteners or enjoy it unsweetened.
  • Enhance flavor with spices like cinnamon or nutmeg without adding calories.
